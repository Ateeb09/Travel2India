Kolkata

The City of Joy—colonial grandeur, literary heritage, and Bengal's passionate soul.

Kolkata is India's cultural capital—a city of poets, revolutionaries, and relentless spirit. Colonial architecture lines grand avenues; Howrah Bridge spans the Hooghly; and tram cars still rattle through neighborhoods. From the Victoria Memorial to Durga Puja pandals, from sweet shops to intellectual adda (conversation), Kolkata wears its history and heart on its sleeve. Experience the warmth of Bengali hospitality, savor rasgulla and fish curry, and discover why it's called the City of Joy.

Must-See

  • Victoria Memorial
  • Howrah Bridge & flower market
  • Indian Museum (oldest in India)
  • Kali Temple (Dakshineswar)
  • Park Street, College Street (books & culture)

Best Time to Visit

  • Oct–Mar: Pleasant weather
  • Oct (Navratri): Durga Puja—Kolkata's biggest festival
  • Avoid: Apr–Jun (hot & humid), Jul–Sep (monsoon)

Suggested Duration

  • 2–3 days: Key monuments, markets, food
  • 4–5 days: Add day trip to Sunderbans or Shantiniketan
  • Gateway to East India—Darjeeling, Sikkim, Odisha
