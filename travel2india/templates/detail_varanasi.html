Varanasi

The spiritual heart of India—one of the world's oldest living cities, where the Ganges holds eternity.

Varanasi, or Kashi, has drawn pilgrims and seekers for over 3,000 years. The sacred Ganges flows past ancient ghats where rituals of life and death unfold daily—sunrise boat rides, evening aartis, and the timeless rhythm of devotion. Wander the maze of lanes behind the ghats, discover silk weavers and yoga schools, and witness a city that has outlived empires. Varanasi is not merely a destination; it's an encounter with the soul of India.

Must-See

  • Sunrise boat ride on the Ganges
  • Evening Ganga Aarti (Dasashwamedh Ghat)
  • Sarnath (Buddhist ruins, Dhamek Stupa)
  • Walk the ghats & old city lanes
  • Silk weaving & Banarasi saree workshops

Best Time to Visit

  • Oct–Mar: Pleasant, ideal for early boat rides
  • Nov: Dev Deepawali (festival of lights on ghats)
  • Avoid: Apr–Jun (extreme heat), Jul–Aug (monsoon floods)

Suggested Duration

  • 2 days: Boat ride, aarti, Sarnath, old city
  • 3 days: Add yoga, silk tour, evening walks
  • Often paired with Bodh Gaya or Ayodhya
